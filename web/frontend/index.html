Descending Neurons (Output)

GF Escape/Flight — The "panic button." When danger is detected, these neurons fire to make the fly jump and fly away. The fastest pathway in the brain.

P9 Forward — Makes the fly walk forward. The hungrier the fly, the more these fire.

MDN Backward — Makes the fly walk backwards (moonwalk). Used when the fly bumps into something.

Turn L/R — Steers the fly left or right. When one side fires more, the fly turns.

aDN1 Groom — Triggers cleaning behavior. The fly rubs its front legs over its face and antennae.

MN9 Feed — Extends the proboscis (drinking tube) and starts feeding when food is found.

Dopamine & Valence

PAM Reward — The "this is good!" signal (~295 dopamine neurons). Fires when the fly finds sugar or food. Teaches the brain to approach.

PPL1 Punishment — The "this is bad!" signal (16 dopamine neurons). Fires before PAM during threats — the brain checks for danger before reward (threat-first architecture).

MBON Approach — Decision neurons that say "go towards it." Strengthened by PAM reward signals.

MBON Avoidance — Decision neurons that say "stay away." Strengthened by PPL1 punishment signals.

MBON Suppress — Referee neurons that resolve conflicts when both approach and avoid are active.

Neuromodulators

5-HT (Serotonin) — The "mood" chemical. Calms the brain during stress, helps with grooming. Spikes when bad smells are detected.

OA (Octopamine) — The insect version of adrenaline. Wakes the brain up, boosts alertness and fight-or-flight response.

Bulk NT Activity

GABA — The brain's brake pedal (~15,000 neurons). Stops other neurons from firing. Without it, the brain would have seizures.

ACh (Acetylcholine) — The brain's gas pedal (~40,000 neurons). The main "go" signal. Nearly half of all brain cells use this.

Glut (Glutamate) — A versatile messenger (~50,000 neurons). Can speed up or slow down signals depending on the circuit.
